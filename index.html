History

The history of this small village goes back to colonial times when Morocco was divided between French and Spanish controls.‎ ‎Mirleft was under French occupation and the military used the heights that dominate this region for surveillance and control. Two kilometres further south, the Marabout beach served as a border between the French and the Spanish.‎ ‎Built in 1935, the remains of the Tidli fort, perched above Mirleft, bear witness to this period.‎ ‎Then, in the 1970s, the village was particularly appreciated by the hippie community.‎

Geography

Mirleft is a rural municipality in the province of Sidi Ifni, in the administrative region of Guelmim-Oued Noun, located in southern Morocco 130 km from Agadir, between the towns of Tiznit and Sidi Ifni, it meets the mountain range of the Anti-Atlas and the Atlantic Ocean. This geological phenomenon has created high cliffs plunging into the ocean, a multitude of wadis that form small beaches at each mouth, and a hinterland formed by large hills populated with cactus , palm trees and argan trees.
